Beginner walkthrough

  1. Open sample_food_products.csv in a spreadsheet.
  2. Identify columns such as product_name, categories, sugars_100g, salt_100g, and saturated_fat_100g.
  3. Improve a vague question into a project question: In this small sample, which product categories show higher sugar, salt, or saturated-fat values, and what limitations should we mention?
  4. Ask AI for risks before analysis.
  5. Paste 10-15 rows into AI and ask it to separate facts, assumptions, hypotheses, and manual checks.
  6. Check one AI claim manually in the spreadsheet.
  7. Rewrite the claim cautiously.

Manual check example

If AI says that one broad category appears higher in sugar or saturated fat, check visible rows directly in the spreadsheet. Do not rely on AI memory or invented averages.

Safer wording: In this small sample, several products in the selected category show high sugar or saturated-fat values. This is only a first screening and cannot be generalized without a larger, more balanced sample.

Example decision brief summary

Objective: inspect a small public food-products dataset and identify categories that show higher sugar, salt, or saturated-fat values.

Finding: in this sample, some categories show higher sugar values, while some prepared or sauce-like products show higher salt values.

Limitations: the sample is small, categories are mixed, and products are not directly comparable. The dataset cannot support broad nutrition advice.

Recommendation: for a real project, select one or two comparable product categories and collect a larger sample before making a decision.
