# 01 - Orientation

## What this mini-course is about

You will learn a practical workflow for using AI in student projects without blindly trusting AI output.

The course uses a real-world food-products dataset. You will ask a project question, inspect the data, use an AI assistant, validate the answer, and write a short decision brief.

This is not a nutrition course. It is not a programming course. It is a project-work course using AI and data.

## The scenario

Imagine a student project team is preparing a simple product-analysis report for a food retailer, student canteen, or consumer-awareness project.

The team has a small dataset of food products with nutrition fields. The team wants to answer:

> Which product category appears less healthy based on available nutrition fields, and how confident can we be given missing or inconsistent data?

Your job is not to produce a perfect scientific answer. Your job is to show a careful project workflow:

1. define the question;
2. understand the data;
3. use AI to help structure the analysis;
4. validate the AI output;
5. communicate the decision and limitations.

## What you will produce

By the end, you should have:

1. A short project decision brief.
2. An AI-use log.
3. A validation checklist.

Optional technical students can also produce a small analysis table using Python.

## What counts as good work

Good work is not the most beautiful AI answer.

Good work means:

- you can explain what you did;
- you know what data you used;
- you can say what AI helped with;
- you checked the AI output;
- you did not hide uncertainty;
- you wrote a clear recommendation for a team.

## What not to do

Do not paste the dataset into an AI tool and ask: "Tell me the answer."

Do not submit an AI-generated answer without checking it.

Do not upload private, personal, patient, unpublished, or company-confidential data into public AI tools.

Do not claim that the dataset proves medical or nutritional truth. The sample is small and partly messy by design.

## The core workflow

Use this workflow throughout the mini-course:

```text
1. Question
   What decision are we trying to support?

2. Context
   What data do we have? What is missing?

3. AI assistance
   What can AI help us structure, summarize, or critique?

4. Validation
   What must humans check?

5. Decision brief
   What should the team do next?
```
