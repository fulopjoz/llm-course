# Example decision brief

This is an example only. Students should not copy it. They should write their own brief based on their own checks.

## Title

First screening of nutrition patterns in a small food-products sample

## 1. Objective

The objective was to inspect a small public food-products dataset and identify which product categories show higher sugar, salt, or saturated-fat values. The goal was to support a cautious project decision, not to make medical or dietary recommendations.

## 2. Data used

I used the provided CSV sample with 30 products. The dataset includes product names, brands, categories, countries, Nutri-Score grades, energy, sugar, salt, saturated fat, protein, and ingredients text.

## 3. Method

I first opened the CSV in a spreadsheet and inspected the columns and product categories. I then used an AI chat assistant to help identify possible patterns and data-quality risks. I manually checked the main claims against the spreadsheet before using them in this brief.

## 4. Findings

In this small sample, several snack and spread products show higher sugar values than categories such as dairy, legumes, or mineral water. Some snack products also show higher saturated-fat values.

Examples checked manually:

- Mars Bar: sugar 59.5 g/100g, saturated fat 8.1 g/100g.
- Kinder Chocolate: sugar 53.5 g/100g, saturated fat 22.6 g/100g.
- Nutella: sugar 56.3 g/100g, saturated fat 10.6 g/100g.
- Mineral water: sugar 0 g/100g, saturated fat 0 g/100g.

Sauces and some ready meals show higher salt values than many sweet products. For example, Barilla Pesto Genovese has salt 3.2 g/100g, and Olomoucke Tvaruzky has salt 4.8 g/100g.

## 5. Limitations

The sample is small and not representative. Product categories are mixed, and drinks, spreads, cheese, snacks, soups, and oils are not directly comparable. Some values may be easier to interpret within a category than across all products. The dataset does not support medical claims or final nutrition recommendations.

## 6. Recommendation

For a real project, the next step should be to select one or two comparable product categories and collect a larger sample. The team should define clear criteria before comparing products, for example sugar for sweet snacks or salt for sauces and ready meals.

## 7. AI-use log

AI tool used: AI chat assistant.

What AI helped with:

- listing data-quality risks;
- suggesting cautious wording;
- organizing the decision brief structure.

What I checked manually:

- product categories;
- sugar values for selected snack/spread examples;
- salt values for selected sauce/dairy examples;
- whether claims were too broad.

What I changed after validation:

- I replaced broad statements such as "snacks are unhealthy" with cautious statements such as "in this small sample, several snack products show higher sugar and saturated-fat values."

Remaining limitation:

- The analysis is only a first screening and should not be used as general nutrition advice.
