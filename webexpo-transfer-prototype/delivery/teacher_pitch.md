# Teacher pitch

## Activity title

**AI for student projects: from messy information to reliable decisions**

## What this activity is

This is a short practical activity for students in the Technology Skills for Project Managers course.

Students work with a small real-world food-products dataset, use one AI chat assistant, validate the AI output manually, and prepare a short project decision brief.

The activity is not a nutrition lesson and not a coding lesson. It is a practical AI/data/project-management exercise.

## Why it fits Technology Skills for Project Managers

The activity combines several course-relevant skills:

- AI literacy;
- spreadsheet-based data inspection;
- prompt design;
- validation of AI output;
- communication of uncertainty;
- project decision-making;
- optional technical extension with Python and coding assistants.

It gives students a concrete way to practice AI use while avoiding the common mistake of copying AI-generated text without checking it.

## Why it is useful for mixed-specialization students

The required route is no-code.

Students only need:

- Excel, Google Sheets, or LibreOffice Calc;
- one AI chat assistant such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude;
- the provided CSV file.

The dataset is accessible to different specializations:

- chemistry students can discuss nutrition fields and ingredients;
- business students can discuss product categories and consumer decisions;
- IT students can discuss data quality;
- design students can discuss clear communication;
- bioinformatics/data students can discuss validation and structured analysis;
- project-management students can discuss risks, assumptions, and next actions.

## What students produce

Each student or group produces:

1. one-page decision brief;
2. AI-use log;
3. validation checklist with at least three limitations or risks.

Optional technical students may also produce:

- a Python-generated summary table;
- a short note on how a coding assistant helped them understand or check the script.

## Required tools

Required:

- spreadsheet tool;
- AI chat assistant;
- provided CSV dataset.

Not required:

- GitHub;
- Python;
- Codex;
- Claude Code;
- GitHub Copilot;
- Cursor;
- paid AI tools.

## Connection to WebExpo

The activity connects to WebExpo themes around:

- AI and messy data;
- reducing analytics noise;
- preparing projects and codebases for AI agents;
- AI in product/project management;
- evidence-led decisions;
- human validation of AI-generated output.

The intended transfer from WebExpo is not a passive summary of talks. It is a reusable classroom exercise that turns conference ideas into a practical student workflow.

## Why it avoids overengineering

The first version is deliberately simple.

Students do not need a web app, GitHub account, or coding setup. The materials can be delivered through Teams or Google Drive as PDFs, a CSV file, a checklist, and a decision brief template.

The GitHub repository remains the source of truth for versioning and optional technical extension, but the student-facing course can be used through normal course platforms.

A static website or guided web app can be added later if the activity proves useful, but it is not necessary for the first delivery.

## Short pitch

This activity gives students a realistic way to practice AI use in project work: they inspect real data, ask AI for support, validate the output, and communicate a cautious decision. It is accessible to non-coders, useful for mixed-specialization teams, and directly aligned with the course goal of building practical technology skills for project managers.
