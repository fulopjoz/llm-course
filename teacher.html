Purpose

This activity transfers WebExpo themes into a practical classroom exercise: students inspect real data, use AI responsibly, validate output, and communicate a cautious project decision.

Why it fits Technology Skills for Project Managers

  • AI literacy.
  • Spreadsheet-based data inspection.
  • Prompt design.
  • Validation of AI output.
  • Communication of uncertainty.
  • Project decision-making.

40-minute lesson plan

  1. 0-5 min: framing. AI is not the deliverable; the decision brief is.
  2. 5-10 min: open the dataset and inspect columns.
  3. 10-18 min: use the first AI prompt.
  4. 18-25 min: validate one AI claim manually.
  5. 25-32 min: draft a decision brief.
  6. 32-37 min: mention optional technical route.
  7. 37-40 min: wrap-up discussion.

Assessment criteria

CriterionEvidence
Clear project questionThe brief states the question clearly.
Data inspectionStudent refers to columns, rows, or checked examples.
Responsible AI useAI-use log says what AI was asked to do.
ValidationAt least one AI claim was checked manually.
LimitationsAt least three limitations or risks are listed.
Cautious wordingStudent avoids unsupported broad claims.
Decision focusBrief includes recommendation or next action.

Required tools

Spreadsheet, one AI chat assistant, and the CSV dataset. GitHub and coding tools are not required.

Delivery guidance

Share this site URL with students. For offline or Teams delivery, provide the dataset CSV and decision brief template from the downloads section.
